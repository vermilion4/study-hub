cards:
  - q: "In one sentence, what is a large language model (LLM)?"
    a: "A neural network trained on large amounts of text to predict the next token, which lets it generate and transform natural language."
  - q: "What does it mean that an LLM is 'probabilistic'?"
    a: "It predicts likely next tokens rather than looking up facts, so the same prompt can yield different outputs and it can be confidently wrong."
  - q: "What is a 'token' in the context of an LLM?"
    a: "A chunk of text (roughly a word-piece) that the model reads and generates; prompts and outputs are measured in tokens."
  - q: "What is a 'hallucination'?"
    a: "When the model produces fluent, plausible-sounding output that is factually wrong or made up."
  - q: "Why does an LLM have a 'context window'?"
    a: "It can only attend to a limited number of tokens at once, so very long inputs must be truncated, summarized, or chunked."
